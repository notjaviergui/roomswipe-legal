Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
Last Updated: May 13, 2026

Introduction
This Data Processing Agreement (the "DPA") forms an integral part of the main service agreement (the "Principal Agreement") entered into between Roomswipe, a website and app operating at https://roomswipe.app ("Roomswipe", "we", "us", or "Processor"), and the client entity ("Client", "you", or "Controller"). This DPA addresses the processing of Personal Data by Roomswipe on behalf of the Client in connection with the services provided under the Principal Agreement.
When you use Roomswipe's services, you might share or allow us to access certain personal information about your users or customers. This document sets out our mutual obligations regarding that data. It's about ensuring we handle that data responsibly and in line with applicable privacy laws, especially those relevant in the United States.

Definitions
For the purposes of this DPA, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

    "Applicable Data Protection Law" means any law, statute, declaration, decree, directive, legislative instrument, order, ordinance, regulation, rule or other binding instrument relating to the protection of Personal Data in force from time to time in the United States, including, but not limited to, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), and other state-specific privacy laws.
    "Controller" means the natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data. In this DPA, the Client is the Controller.
    "Data Subject" means the identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates. This could be a user of your services, a customer, or anyone whose data you provide to Roomswipe.
    "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that Roomswipe processes on behalf of the Controller in connection with the provision of the Services. This includes, for example, names, email addresses, IP addresses, or any other data that can identify an individual.
    "Processing" means any operation or set of operations which is performed on Personal Data or on sets of Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organization, structuring, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.
    "Processor" means a natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. In this DPA, Roomswipe is the Processor.
    "Services" means the services provided by Roomswipe to the Client as described in the Principal Agreement, which may involve the processing of Personal Data.
    "Sub-processor" means any third party engaged by Roomswipe to process Personal Data on behalf of the Client.


Scope and Applicability of this DPA
This DPA applies to all Personal Data processed by Roomswipe when providing the Services to the Client under the Principal Agreement. It clarifies the roles and responsibilities of both parties regarding data protection. This isn't just a formality; it's a commitment to protecting the privacy of individuals whose data we handle.
Specifically, this DPA covers any data you, as the Controller, provide to Roomswipe through our website at https://roomswipe.app or via our app operations. This includes data necessary for account management, service delivery, and any other functions where Roomswipe acts as a data processor.

Roles of the Parties
The parties acknowledge and agree that for the purposes of the Applicable Data Protection Law:

    The Client is the Controller of the Personal Data. You determine why and how the data is processed.
    Roomswipe is the Processor of the Personal Data. We process the data only on your behalf and according to your instructions.

This distinction is important. As the Controller, you bear the primary responsibility for the lawfulness of the data you provide to us. We, as the Processor, are responsible for handling that data securely and as instructed.

Details of Processing
The parties agree that the details of the processing activities are as follows:

Subject Matter of the Processing
The subject matter of the processing is the Personal Data submitted by the Client to Roomswipe in connection with the Services provided under the Principal Agreement. This includes, for example, user account information, preferences, or other data necessary for the Roomswipe platform to function for your users.

Duration of the Processing
Roomswipe will process Personal Data for the duration of the Principal Agreement, unless otherwise agreed in writing. We'll also process it as long as necessary to comply with legal obligations or to resolve disputes, even after the agreement ends.

Nature and Purpose of the Processing
The nature of the processing involves the collection, storage, organization, structuring, use, and disclosure of Personal Data as required to provide the Services. The purpose is to enable Roomswipe to deliver the functionalities and services outlined in the Principal Agreement to the Client and its users. This might include managing user accounts, facilitating communication, or providing analytics related to your use of https://roomswipe.app.

Categories of Personal Data
The categories of Personal Data processed may include, but are not limited to:

    Identification data (e.g., names, usernames, email addresses, phone numbers)
    Contact data (e.g., mailing addresses, billing addresses)
    Technical data (e.g., IP addresses, device information, browser type, usage data, cookies)
    Financial data (e.g., payment information, if applicable to the services)
    Any other data voluntarily submitted by the Client or its Data Subjects through the Roomswipe platform.


Categories of Data Subjects
The categories of Data Subjects whose Personal Data is processed may include, but are not limited to:

    The Client's end-users or customers
    The Client's employees or representatives
    Any other individuals whose Personal Data is provided by the Client to Roomswipe.


Processor's Obligations (Roomswipe)

Compliance with Instructions
Roomswipe will process Personal Data only on the documented instructions from the Client, including with regard to transfers of Personal Data to a third country or an international organization, unless required to do so by Applicable Data Protection Law. If we are required to process data by law, we'll inform you of that legal requirement before processing, unless the law prohibits such notification.

Confidentiality
Roomswipe ensures that persons authorized to process the Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality. Our team understands the sensitive nature of the data we handle on your behalf.

Security Measures
Roomswipe implements appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, taking into account the state of the art, the costs of implementation, and the nature, scope, context, and purposes of processing as well as the risk of varying likelihood and severity for the rights and freedoms of natural persons. This means we use industry-standard security practices to protect the data you entrust to us through https://roomswipe.app. These measures include, but are not limited to:

    Encryption of Personal Data in transit and at rest where appropriate.
    Access controls to prevent unauthorized access to systems processing Personal Data.
    Regular security assessments and vulnerability scanning.
    Incident response plans to address potential security breaches.


Sub-processing
The Client generally authorizes Roomswipe to engage Sub-processors. Roomswipe will inform the Client of any intended changes concerning the addition or replacement of Sub-processors, thereby giving the Client the opportunity to object to such changes. If you object, we'll work with you to find a solution. If we can't, either party may terminate the Principal Agreement.
When Roomswipe engages a Sub-processor, we will impose on that Sub-processor data protection obligations that are substantially similar to those set out in this DPA. We remain fully liable to the Client for the performance of the Sub-processor's obligations.

Assistance to Controller (Data Subject Rights)
Taking into account the nature of the processing, Roomswipe will assist the Client by appropriate technical and organizational measures, insofar as this is possible, for the fulfillment of the Client's obligation to respond to requests for exercising the Data Subject's rights under Applicable Data Protection Law. This means if a user of your service asks to access or delete their data, we'll help you facilitate that request.

Data Breach Notification
Roomswipe will notify the Client without undue delay upon becoming aware of a Personal Data breach affecting the Personal Data processed under this DPA. We'll provide the Client with sufficient information to meet any obligations to report or inform Data Subjects of the Personal Data breach. This includes details about the nature of the breach, the categories of data involved, and the measures taken to address it.

Assistance with Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)
Roomswipe will provide reasonable assistance to the Client with any data protection impact assessments and prior consultations with supervisory authorities that the Client is required to carry out under Applicable Data Protection Law, but only to the extent that such assistance is necessary and relates to the processing of Personal Data by Roomswipe.

Deletion or Return of Data
Upon termination or expiration of the Principal Agreement, Roomswipe will, at the Client's choice, delete or return all Personal Data to the Client and delete existing copies, unless Applicable Data Protection Law requires storage of the Personal Data. We'll confirm in writing when this has been completed.

Controller's Obligations (Client)
As the Controller, you have specific responsibilities under this DPA and Applicable Data Protection Law:

    You warrant that you have all necessary rights, consents, and permissions to provide the Personal Data to Roomswipe for processing. This means you've obtained any required consent from your users or have another lawful basis for sharing their data with us.
    You are responsible for the accuracy, quality, and legality of the Personal Data provided to Roomswipe.
    You will ensure that your instructions to Roomswipe for the processing of Personal Data comply with Applicable Data Protection Law.
    You will notify Roomswipe promptly of any changes in the purpose or scope of processing that might affect our obligations under this DPA.


Audits and Inspections
Roomswipe will make available to the Client all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations laid down in this DPA and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Client or another auditor mandated by the Client. This is subject to reasonable notice, confidentiality obligations, and at the Client's expense.
We understand the need for transparency. However, such audits must not unreasonably interfere with Roomswipe's business operations or compromise the security of other clients' data. We'll work with you to find a practical approach.

International Data Transfers
Roomswipe operates primarily within the United States. However, in providing the Services, Personal Data may be transferred to, and stored and processed in, other countries where Roomswipe or its Sub-processors maintain facilities. This is common with cloud-based services like https://roomswipe.app.
When such transfers occur, Roomswipe will ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place to protect the Personal Data in accordance with Applicable Data Protection Law. This might involve using standard contractual clauses or relying on other recognized transfer mechanisms.

Liability
Each party's liability under this DPA is subject to the limitations of liability set forth in the Principal Agreement. Neither party will be liable for any indirect, incidental, special, consequential, or punitive damages, or any loss of profits or revenues, whether incurred directly or indirectly, or any loss of data, use, goodwill, or other intangible losses, resulting from the processing activities under this DPA.

Term and Termination
This DPA will become effective upon the effective date of the Principal Agreement and will remain in force until the Principal Agreement expires or is terminated. Upon termination of the Principal Agreement, this DPA will automatically terminate.
The provisions of this DPA that by their nature are intended to survive termination (e.g., confidentiality, data deletion obligations) will remain in effect.

Governing Law
This DPA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the United States, without regard to its conflict of law principles. Any disputes arising under or in connection with this DPA shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts located in the United States.

Miscellaneous
Entire Agreement
This DPA, together with the Principal Agreement, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior discussions, negotiations, and agreements, whether written or oral.

Amendments
Any amendments to this DPA must be in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both parties. We can't just change things on a whim; it requires mutual agreement.

Severability
If any provision of this DPA is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions will remain in full force and effect. The invalid or unenforceable provision will be replaced by a valid and enforceable provision that most closely reflects the intent of the original provision.
